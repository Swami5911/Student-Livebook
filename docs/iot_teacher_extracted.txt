🎤 FULL TEACHING SCRIPT (WORD-BY-WORD GUIDANCE)
Opening (0–5 min)
“Good morning everyone. Before we begin, tell me—how many of you use a smartphone, smartwatch, or smart TV?” (Pause; take responses) “Have you noticed how these devices connect to the internet and sometimes act automatically? This is not magic. This is a technology called the Internet of Things, or IoT.”
Introduction & Definition (5–20 min)
“In earlier days, devices performed only one task. A fan needed a switch. A light needed a person. Today, devices are connected and intelligent.”
“The Internet of Things means connecting physical objects—like devices, machines, or appliances—to the internet so they can send data, receive data, and act automatically.”
Write on board:
Internet of Things (IoT):
Network of physical objects embedded with sensors,
software, and connectivity to collect and exchange data.
“In simple words, IoT helps devices sense, think, and act.”
Keyword-by-Keyword Explanation (20–35 min)
Physical Objects
“Physical objects are real-world items—phones, ACs, cars, machines.”
Sensors
“Sensors are devices that detect changes like temperature, motion, or light.”
Software
“Software analyzes the data and decides what action to take.”
Communication Technologies
“Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and mobile networks help devices communicate.”
Automation
“Automation means devices work without human help.”
Ask:
“Can you think of an example where something works automatically at home?”
How IoT Works (35–55 min)
“IoT works in a cycle.”
Write and explain:
Sensing → Transmission → Processing → Decision → Action
“This cycle repeats continuously.”
Use example:
“A smart light senses darkness, sends data to the cloud, software decides, and the light turns ON.”
Components of IoT (55–70 min)
Explain one-by-one:
Sensors (data collection)
Actuators (action)
Controller (decision-making)
Connectivity (communication)
Cloud (storage & analytics)
Application (user interface)
“Remember—sensors collect data, actuators take action.”
Applications & Challenges (70–80 min)
“IoT is used in homes, healthcare, cities, industries, and agriculture.”
“But IoT also faces challenges like security, privacy, and data management.”
Ask:
“Why do you think security is important in IoT?”
Recap (80–90 min)
“Today we learned what IoT is, how it works, and where it is used. This forms the foundation for the entire course.”
